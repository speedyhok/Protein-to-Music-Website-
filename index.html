🧬 Protein → Music

Hear what disease-causing mutations sound like — generated live using ESM-2 embeddings.

Every protein in your body is a chain of amino acids — and sometimes, a single letter in that chain changes. That's a mutation. Some are harmless. Others cause diseases like sickle cell anemia, cystic fibrosis, or cancer.

ESM-2 is an AI model trained on hundreds of millions of protein sequences from across all life on Earth. It learned, just from patterns in evolution, which amino acid changes are "normal" and which ones are deeply unusual — without ever being told what a disease is.

This tool takes a healthy protein and its disease-causing mutant version, runs both through ESM-2, and turns the AI's internal representations into music. The chart below shows exactly how much the AI "notices" the mutation at each position — usually a sharp spike right where the disease-causing change occurs.
